WHAT ARE HATE CRIMES?

In the current legislation, hate crime is defined as a crime that is motivated by malicious or ill will toward a social group. Consistent hate crime can have devastating impacts on individuals, families, communities, and the very fabric of society. There are five core "protected characteristics", which cover all aspects of an individual's life, including their race, religion, disabilities, sexual orientation, and transgender identity. This can be verbal abuse, criminal damage, violence, sexual assault, and murder. In general, hate crime is not always committed by strangers, since many victims know the perpetrator(s) such as their own family members or close friends.

RACE HATE CRIME

Defining race-related hate crimes can be acts of violence or hostility directed at individuals because of who they are. Race hate crimes include verbal and physical abuse, bullying, online bullying, threatening behavior, or damaging property (vandalization). A race-related hate crime is when someone is targeted because of their "race, color, nationality, ethnicity, or national origin", according to Citizens Advice. If the victim thinks the crime is motivated by hostility or prejudice based on race, this could be considered a hate crime. All in all, a race related hate crime is an act thats is based apon their physical characteristics. Such as, their race and colour.

Ways in which race related hate crimes occur
  • Physical abuse - intentional acts causing injury or trauma to another person of bodily contact (Assault and Battery).
  • Verbal abuse - is a type of psychological/mental abuse that involves the use of oral language, gestured language, and written language directed at a victim. Verbal abuse can include the act of harassing, labeling, insulting, scolding, rebuking, excessive yelling towards an individual.

Various degrees of why race-related hate crime occur
  • Race
  • Color
  • Nationality
  • Ethnicity
  • National orgin

RELIGION HATE CRIME

An act of religious based hate crime is an act that targets a victim or a group in a hostile way based on their religious or theological beliefs. These crimes are sometimes referred to as sectarian crimes. For instance, crimes motivated by inter-faith or intra-faith hostility or crimes targeting someone who has left a particular religion are investigated and prosecuted as religious hate crimes. They could attract the same enhanced sentencing in court. If the victim believes that a crime was motivated by religious hostility, it will be recorded. If the offender is to get a sentence enhanced by the court, prosecutors must provide evidence to prove it, but it is not required for recording purposes. All in all, Human rights principles are fundamental to all religious beliefs, meaning everyone has a right to hold their religious beliefs and to practice their theological faith in safety. An atheist has the same rights to live free from targeted abuse as does a believer in a majority or minority religion.

DISABILITY HATE CRIME

Disability hate crimes are motivated by prejudice against people with disabilities and complex needs. Individuals with disabilities or those who are perceived to have disabilities can be targeted due, to being vulnerable due to their impairment or health condition. Prejudicial actions toward people with disabilities can lead to their exclusion within society due to confidence problems. Therefore, prejudice manifests itself in the individual which can lead to dire consequences for individuals such as social interaction and day-to-day life. All in all, the targeting of individuals with disabilities who are considered to be "easy targets" is an expression of bias, and is, therefore, considered a hate crime.
